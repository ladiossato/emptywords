---
title: "How Wanting to Be Great Proves You Never Will Be"
date: 2024-11-13
---

You want to be great.

Of course you do.

Who doesn't want transcendence? Who doesn't hunger for mastery? For recognition? For the feeling of touching something beyond ordinary human capability?

But watch what happens the moment you want it.

Watch very carefully.

Because in that moment of wanting, you have already made greatness impossible.

Not difficult.

Impossible.

And the mechanism by which this occurs is so precise, so elegant, that once you see it operating, you cannot unsee it.

You are sitting there right now, reading these words, and part of you is wanting to be great at something.

Maybe writing. Maybe business. Maybe art. Maybe relationships.

Feel that wanting.

Notice how it sits in your body. The slight tension. The forward lean of your mind toward some imagined future where you possess what you currently lack.

This is the moment. Right here.

This is where greatness dies.

Because wanting creates the wanter.

And the wanter can never be great.

The wanter is the one who stands outside greatness, looking in. The wanter is the one who measures the distance between where they are and where they believe they should be.

The wanter is the one who turns every moment of potential flow into a calculation about progress.

You touch the piano keys and instead of simply touching, you are wanting to play like Chopin.

You write a sentence and instead of simply writing, you are wanting to craft like Hemingway.

You enter a conversation and instead of simply being present, you are wanting to be charismatic, compelling, memorable.

The wanting creates a split.

A split between the one who acts and the one who observes the action for signs of greatness.

This observer, this wanter, is a tyrant.

It whispers constantly: "Is this great yet? How about now? Are we making progress toward greatness?"

And every whisper pulls you out of the moment where greatness actually lives.

Greatness is not a destination.

It is not a thing to be acquired.

It is not even a state to be achieved.

Greatness is what remains when the wanter dissolves.

When there is no one left to want greatness, greatness can express itself through the instrument of your being.

But this is not a technique.

You cannot dissolve the wanter by wanting to dissolve the wanter.

You cannot stop wanting by wanting to stop wanting.

The very effort to eliminate wanting is itself wanting.

And so the trap completes itself.

The harder you try to transcend wanting, the more the wanter strengthens. The more you effort toward greatness, the further you move from it.

You have seen this in others, haven't you?

The musician who practices scales obsessively but never touches music.

The writer who studies craft maniacally but never touches truth.

The businessman who chases strategies frantically but never touches intuition.

They are all caught in the same mechanism.

The wanting has become so strong that it drowns out everything else.

They cannot hear the music for listening for their progress at hearing music.

They cannot see the words for measuring their advancement toward being a writer.

They cannot feel the market for calculating their movement toward being successful.

The wanting creates noise.

And greatness requires silence.

Not the silence of meditation retreats or quiet rooms.

The silence of a mind that has stopped measuring its distance from some imagined ideal.

When Michelangelo carved David, was there a part of him standing aside, wanting to create a masterpiece?

Or was there simply stone and chisel and the inevitable unfolding of what was already present?

When Einstein formulated relativity, was there a part of him wanting to revolutionize physics?

Or was there simply curiosity following itself to its natural conclusion?

When Jordan touched flow on the basketball court, was there a part of him wanting to be great?

Or was there simply basketball happening through him?

The great ones do not want to be great.

They cannot want to be great.

Because the moment wanting enters, they are no longer in the place where greatness lives.

This is why all the books about achieving greatness miss the mark.

They strengthen the very thing that prevents greatness.

They feed the wanter.

They give the wanter more sophisticated strategies, more refined techniques, more detailed roadmaps.

But the wanter, no matter how sophisticated, cannot access greatness.

Because the wanter and greatness cannot occupy the same space.

You are probably feeling frustrated right now.

Because these words point to something you cannot grab.

You cannot make a system out of not wanting.

You cannot create a practice out of dissolving the wanter.

You cannot want your way to not wanting.

And this frustration is the wanter asserting itself.

It wants a method. A technique. A pathway.

It wants to turn even this understanding into another strategy for achieving greatness.

But understanding the trap is not the same as escaping it.

Seeing the mechanism is not the same as transcending it.

The trap remains.

And it remains because it serves a function.

The wanter exists to protect you from the terror of simply being what you are.

Without the wanter, you would have to confront the possibility that you might touch greatness.

And touching greatness is terrifying.

Because when greatness moves through you, there is no you to take credit.

When mastery expresses itself, there is no master to feel proud.

When flow occurs, there is no one left to experience the achievement.

The wanter protects you from this dissolution.

It keeps you safely separate from the very thing you claim to desire.

It gives you the comfort of effort without the risk of actual transcendence.

Because transcendence means the end of the one who wants to transcend.

And so you remain trapped.

Wanting greatness.

Working toward greatness.

Measuring your progress toward greatness.

Never seeing that greatness is what you already are when the wanting stops.

This is the ultimate irony.

You are trying to become what you already are.

You are efforting toward what only appears in the absence of effort.

You are wanting what can only be touched by the one who wants nothing.

The mechanism is perfect.

And perfectly cruel.

Because it uses your highest aspirations against you.

It turns your deepest longing into the very thing that prevents its fulfillment.

Watch it operating in yourself.

Right now.

Feel how even reading these words has triggered the wanter.

Feel how part of you is already trying to figure out how to use this understanding.

How to not want.

How to dissolve the wanter.

How to finally, finally touch greatness.

That trying?

That's the trap.

Operating perfectly.

As it always does.
